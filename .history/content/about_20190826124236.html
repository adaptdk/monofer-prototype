About

The Monofer Dosing Tool allows you to easily identify the most convenient strategy for treating your patients' iron deficiency anaemia. You can select from an individual or a simplified dosing option.

The clinical objective when treating iron deficiency anaemia is expressed as the target Hb increase, that is the difference between the target Hb level and a patient's actual Hb level.
Both dosing options allow you to set the units for Hb that are being used in your clinical practice (g/dl, g/l or mmol/l).
The second important parameter for calculating a patient's iron need is the body weight (kg). Please note that the ideal body weight or pre-pregnancy weight should be used. The body weight is used to assess the iron dose/body weight (mg/kg) ratio.

Individual dosing:

The iron deficit and recommended iron dose is calculated using the Ganzoni formula:
Note that the Ganzoni formula states that for a person with a body weight above 35 kg, the iron stores are 500 mg or above.

Total iron need [mg] = Body weight [kg] · (Target Hb – Actual Hb) [g/dl] · 2.4 + 500 [mg]

It is recommended to use the Ganzoni formula in patients who are likely to require individually adjusted dosing such as patients with anorexia nervosa, cachexia, obesity, pregnancy or anaemia due to bleeding

Simplified dosing:

The simplified dosing option allows you to identify the patient’s iron need based on actual Hb and body weight.
